What is BMI?

BMI stands for Body Mass Index, which is a measure of body fat based on height and weight. It is used to assess whether a person has a healthy body weight for their height. BMI can also indicate the risk of certain health problems, such as diabetes, heart disease, and some cancers.

How to calculate BMI?

BMI is calculated by dividing your weight in kilograms by your height in meters squared. For example, if you weigh 70 kg and are 1.75 m tall, your BMI is 70 / (1.75 x 1.75) = 22.9 kg/m2.

What are the BMI categories?

According to the World Health Organization (WHO), there are five main BMI categories for adults:
  • Underweight: BMI below 18.5 kg/m2
  • Normal weight: BMI between 18.5 and 24.9 kg/m2
  • Overweight: BMI between 25 and 29.9 kg/m2
  • Obese: BMI above 30 kg/m2

These categories may vary depending on factors such as age, sex, ethnicity, and muscle mass.

Perfect Plane Diet:

What is the perfect plane diet?

The perfect plane diet is a diet that helps you maintain or achieve a healthy BMI while traveling by plane. Traveling by plane can be stressful and disruptive for your normal eating habits, especially if you cross different time zones or have long flights. You may experience jet lag, dehydration, bloating, constipation, or cravings for unhealthy foods.


To avoid these problems and keep your weight in check, you should follow some simple tips for your plane diet:

Drink plenty of water before, during, and after your flight. Water helps you stay hydrated, flush out toxins, and prevent overeating. Avoid alcohol, caffeine, and sugary drinks on the plane. These beverages can dehydrate you, disrupt your sleep cycle, and increase your appetite. Choose healthy snacks and meals on the plane or bring your own. Opt for foods that are high in protein, fiber, and healthy fats, such as nuts, seeds, fruits, vegetables, yogurt, cheese, eggs, or lean meat. Avoid foods that are high in salt, sugar, or fat, such as chips, cookies, candy, or fried foods. Eat according to your destinations time zone. This can help you adjust to the new time zone faster and avoid jet lag.


For example: If you are flying from New York to London, you should have breakfast when it is morning in London, even if it is still night in New York. Move around on the plane as much as possible. Sitting for long periods can slow down your metabolism and cause blood clots. Try to get up and walk around every hour or do some stretches in your seat. By following these tips, you can enjoy your plane trip without compromising your health or weight goals.
